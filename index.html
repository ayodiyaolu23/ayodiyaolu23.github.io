Research

I am a PhD candidate in Pharmaceutical Sciences and Molecular Medicine at Washington State University. My research focuses on Allosteric Modulation and Gating Mechanisms of GABAA receptor.

My theses involves applying physics-based computational methods to understand the atomistic details of receptor function, with a particular interest in GABA-A Receptors.

My recent research has focused on the structural basis for allosteric modulation of GABAA receptors by neurosteroids and the structural determinants, pathways, and energetics of GABA binding and unbinding to the GABAA receptors.
