Research

I am a PhD candidate in Pharmaceutical Sciences and Molecular Medicine at Washington State University. My research focuses on Computer Aided Drug Design and Anti-Microbial Susceptibility.

I am an inquisitive soul in search of computational solutions for complex health issues. My work involves applying advanced computational methods to drug discovery and development, with a particular interest in addressing antimicrobial resistance challenges.

My recent research has focused on the structural basis for allosteric modulation of GABAA receptors by neurosteroids and the structural determinants, pathways, and energetics of GABA binding and unbinding to the GABAA receptor. Some of my recent publications include:

  • "Structural basis for the allosteric modulation of GABAA receptors by neurosteroids" (2023) in Biophysical Journal, where we investigated the binding of rubidium ions in KcsA channels and its implications for ion selectivity and conductance.
  • "Structural determinants, pathways, and energetics of GABA binding and unbinding to the GABAA receptor" (2024) in Biophysical Journal, where we explored the molecular interactions and structural changes involved in GABA binding to GABAA receptors using advanced computational techniques like metadynamics and steered molecular dynamics.

These studies contribute to our understanding of ion channel function and neurotransmitter receptor mechanisms, which are crucial for developing new therapeutic approaches in neuropharmacology and drug design.
